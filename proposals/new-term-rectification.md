# [new term] Rectification

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/rectification.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Rectification

**Proposed definition:**

> A dispute outcome in which an adjudicator determines that the party-incident-land triple itself is wrongly stated — the wrong holder registered, an incident existing that should not, a transaction in the chain void or procured by fraud — and restores the lawful position. Rectification operates through two instruments under the adjudicator's power: voiding the wrongly registered transactions (a *status change* on the log) and directing new *VoP*, *VoL*, or *VoI* primitives until the register reflects the position the law recognises as correct. It is therefore not an amendment but an adjudicator-directed sequence of ordinary primitives, bounded by the *dispute* pathway and the adjudicator's empowerment. Its bitemporal signature: rectification does not itself move valid time, though the primitives it directs may establish a new valid-time state. Distinguished from *Correction* (triple intact; content restated) and *Amendment* (party-driven; terms change; valid time moves).

**Redraft note:**

REDRAFTED 2026-06-12: the original draft (from the 2024 LRC ontology) made this custodian-initiated and prejudice-discriminated. The Conceptual Foundation §3.3 overrides: adjudication-driven, triple changes, voids + directed primitives, severity expressly not the discriminator.
