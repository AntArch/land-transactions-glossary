# [new term] Transmission

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/transmission.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Transmission

**Proposed definition:**

> A *VoP* that occurs by operation of law rather than by consensual dealing: the holder's death (passing to personal representatives or heirs), insolvency (vesting in a trustee or liquidator), or dissolution of a corporate holder (passing as bona vacantia or under a court vesting order). Transmission qualifies the *consent principle*: the authorisation for the variation is supplied by statute, court order, or a personal representative rather than by the granting party. The incidents themselves are unchanged — transmission moves the holder, not the content of the bundle.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

VoP currently reads as consensual dealing. Transmission is the involuntary VoP — death, insolvency, dissolution — and it qualifies the Consent principle (consent supplied by statute, court, or personal representative). Source: LRC ontology 'transmission' and 'vestingOrder' (bona vacantia).
