# [new term] Transaction algebra

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/transaction-algebra.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Transaction algebra

**Proposed definition:**

> The framework's answer to how party-incident-land relationships change: three interdependent layers forming a transactional grammar. Transaction principles (the *acceptance criteria*) constrain what is permissible; *transaction primitives* (VoP, VoL, VoI) express what is possible; *pattern libraries* demonstrate what is practical. The algebra distinguishes three world-state operations — variation (changes the triple), *amendment* (terms only, same durable identifier), and *assessment* (ascribes a derived fact) — which are exhaustive at the level of the world-state, plus log-level *status changes* directed through the *dispute* pathway. LADM tells you what exists; the algebra specifies how changes to that state must be formed and governed.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

The CF's own central construct: the three-layer transactional grammar (principles/primitives/patterns) and the three world-state operations. It is the named thing the portfolio contributes over LADM and should be citable by URI. Source: Conceptual Foundation (2026-06-12).
