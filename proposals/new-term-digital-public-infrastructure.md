# [new term] Digital Public Infrastructure

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/digital-public-infrastructure.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Digital Public Infrastructure

**Proposed definition:**

> The shared, foundational digital systems through which states deliver public services at scale: a set of interoperable systems built on open standards providing equitable access to public and private services, distinguishing foundational layers (identity, registries, data exchange) that are invested in once and shared from the application services built on top. The land-transactions framework is the land-sector instantiation: authoritative registers exposed as governed services, bound by shared *durable identifiers* and the *once-only principle*, with transaction logic any programme can compose rather than rebuild. The ecosystem, not the individual agency, is the unit of design.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

The executive summary frames the entire framework as the land-sector view of DPI; the briefs and LinkedIn series will lean on the term constantly. Needs a governed definition with the land-sector reading attached. Source: Conceptual Foundation (2026-06-12).
