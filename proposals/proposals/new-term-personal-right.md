# [new term] Personal right

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/personal-right.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Personal right (Right in personam)

**Proposed definition:**

> A right enforceable against a specific person rather than against the world (in personam): the buyer's right against the seller under contract before completion, a creditor's unsecured claim [@gretton_steven_2017]. Personal rights are not registrable and sit outside the *numerus clausus*; conveyancing is, in one reading, the machinery by which a personal right (the contract) is converted into a *real right* (the registered incident). Contrast *Real right*.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

Pairs with Real right. States why contracts, missives, and unsecured claims do not enter the register, and frames conveyancing as the conversion of a personal right into a real one. Note: the LRC ontology's own comment on 'personalRight' is a copy-paste of 'realRight' and needs fixing in the ontology — the definition proposed here is the corrected one.
