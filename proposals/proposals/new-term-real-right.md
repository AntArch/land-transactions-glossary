# [new term] Real right

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/real-right.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Real right (Right in rem)

**Proposed definition:**

> A right held directly in a thing and enforceable against the world (in rem) [@gretton_steven_2017]. Real rights are the subject matter of registration: the *numerus clausus* is a closed list of recognised real right types, and the register's claims of completeness and *priority* are claims about real rights. Contrast *Personal right*.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

The in rem / in personam distinction silently underpins two existing terms — Numerus clausus applies to real rights, and the register records real rights — but is nowhere stated. Source: LRC ontology 'realRight', grounded in Gretton & Steven (2017).
