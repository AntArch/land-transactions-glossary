# [new term] Overriding interest

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/overriding-interest.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Overriding interest

**Proposed definition:**

> An incident that binds a *land object* without appearing on the register: a class of rights reserved by the jurisdiction, typically by statute, that remain enforceable against acquirers despite non-registration. Membership varies by jurisdiction — short leases, rights of persons in actual occupation, public rights of way, certain customary rights. Overriding interests are the explicit boundary of the *once-only principle* as applied to registers: they mark what the register does not promise to show, and *acceptance criteria* cannot test against entries that are not there. A register-completeness claim is only as strong as the enumerated list of interests that override it.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

Names the explicit boundary of the once-only/register-completeness claim: the class of incidents that bind without registration. Acceptance criteria cannot test against entries that are not there; a framework promising 'the register is the source' is stronger for enumerating the exception class.
