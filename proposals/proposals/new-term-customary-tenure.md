# [new term] Customary and informal tenure

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/customary-tenure.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Customary and informal tenure (Informal tenure, Customary right)

**Proposed definition:**

> Rights held and administered under the customs of a community — frequently communal, often unwritten, and in a growing number of jurisdictions carrying formal legal status. The transaction model accommodates customary tenure rather than excluding it: *VoL* is tenure-agnostic and operates on non-crisp spatial units defined relationally, by sketch, or by text (as LADM's social-tenure profile allows), and *first registration* formalises an existing customary or possessory holding through an ordinary *VoI* derivation whose authorised source is the formalisation statute or the proven prior holding. The scope boundary therefore falls not at customary tenure as such but at un-formalised dealings conducted outside the register, which the algebra cannot see until a formalisation pathway brings them in.

**Redraft note:**

REDRAFTED 2026-06-12: the original draft scope-excluded customary tenure. The Conceptual Foundation overrides: VoL is tenure-agnostic (social-tenure profile) and first registration formalises customary holdings; the boundary is formalisation status, not tenure type.
