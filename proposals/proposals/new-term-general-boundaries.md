# [new term] General boundaries

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/general-boundaries.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** General boundaries (Fixed boundaries)

**Proposed definition:**

> A boundary-fixation policy under which the register records the approximate extent of a *spatial unit*: the line shown on the mapbase indicates but does not determine the exact legal boundary, which remains a matter of the underlying instruments and physical features. The alternative is fixed (coordinated) boundaries, where the register's geometry is itself authoritative. The policy in force conditions what *VoL* operations can claim and what the *cadastral index* warrants: under general boundaries the index's geometry is a derived view of evidence, not the evidence itself.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

The boundary-fixation policy in force conditions what VoL can claim and what the Cadastral index warrants. England & Wales operates on general boundaries; the glossary's spatial claims should be explicit about which regime they assume. Source: LRC ontology 'relativeLand'.
