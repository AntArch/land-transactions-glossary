# [new term] First registration

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/first-registration.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** First registration (First grant)

**Proposed definition:**

> The composite by which a land object and its initial incident first enter the register: a *VoL* originates the spatial unit, followed by the operation the incident's jural source determines. Where the state is the effective prior holder (state land disposed of in a planned development), the second step is a *VoP* from the state's own holding, the state's disposition supplying both power and consent. Where a holder already exists under customary, possessory, or informal tenure, the second step is a *VoI* derivation whose authorised source is the formalisation statute or the proven prior holding, derivation and vesting collapsing into a single act in the existing holder's hands. The absence of a prior registered holder does not mean the land is unowned; what varies is the jural source of the initial incident, not the primitives. First registration is the bootstrap that *good root of title* presupposes: before it, validity rests on the instrument chain; after it, the register's versioned record carries the burden.

**Redraft note:**

REDRAFTED 2026-06-12: the original draft was a simple narrower-of-derivation. The Conceptual Foundation stress test overrides: a VoL-first composite whose second step (VoP from the state's holding, or formalising VoI) is determined by the jural source.
