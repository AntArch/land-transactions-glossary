# [new term] Dispute

*Paste into a **New term proposal** issue. Term file already drafted at `terms/dispute.yaml` (status: draft).*

**Proposed term:** Dispute (Dispute pathway, Objection)

**Proposed definition:**

> A transaction any affected party may submit against a specified party-incident-land position, routed through the appropriate objection pathway (valuation appeal, planning appeal, boundary or title dispute). A dispute requires no operation the algebra does not already contain: if upheld, the adjudicating body is empowered to direct a *correction* (an adjudicated amendment), a *rectification* (adjudicator-directed primitives with log-level voids as their instrument), or a directed *assessment*. The dispute pathway is the second source of power in the change matrix: entitled actors change the world through the gates; adjudicators change it through disputes.

**Why the glossary needs it:**

The change matrix has two power sources: entitled actors through the gates, adjudicators through the dispute pathway. The glossary defines the gates but not the pathway; correction and rectification both depend on it. Source: Conceptual Foundation (2026-06-12).
